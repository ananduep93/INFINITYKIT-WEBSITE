Understanding Your Network Performance

Internet speed is a critical factor in how we experience the digital world. Whether you are streaming high-definition video, playing online games, or working from home, a slow connection can cause immense frustration. This tool helps you measure your performance.

We measure speed in Mbps (Megabits per second). It's important to understand the difference between download speed (loading content) and upload speed (sending content), as well as latency (the delay in communication).

🛠️

How to Use the Speed Test

  1. Log In: To save your speed test history or run advanced tests, please log in.
  2. Click the Start Speed Test button.
  3. The tool will first measure your ping (latency).
  4. It will then simulate downloading a file to calculate your download speed.
  5. View your final results on the gauge.
✨

Key Features & Benefits

  • Instant Results: No waiting for minutes. Get results in seconds.
  • Ping Measurement: Crucial for gamers and video callers to know their delay.
  • History Tracking: Logged-in users can see if their ISP is providing consistent speeds over time.
  • Lightweight: No heavy flash players or Java applets required. Works on mobile.
💡

What Do the Numbers Mean?

How to interpret your results:

⬇️ Download Speed

1-5 Mbps is okay for basic browsing. 25+ Mbps is needed for 4K streaming. 100+ Mbps is excellent for families.

⏱️ Ping (Latency)

Under 20ms is great for gaming. 20-50ms is good. Over 100ms will cause noticeable delays in calls.

Why a Login is Required for History

Running a single speed test is a stateless action. However, tracking your speed over a week to prove to your ISP that they are throttling you requires a database to store those results tied to an account. We require a login for these features to:

  • Generate Trend Reports: See graphs of your speed at different times of the day.
  • Test Upload Speed: Uploading test files requires secure server endpoints to prevent spam.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: How does it work? ▼
The tool downloads a small file from our servers and measures how long it takes to calculate your download speed.
Q: Is login required to use the tool? ▼
Yes, a free account is required to save your speed test history or to run advanced tests like upload speed and packet loss. Basic testing is free.
Q: Why is my result different from Speedtest.net? ▼
Different tools use different server locations. Our tool measures the speed to our specific content delivery network nodes.

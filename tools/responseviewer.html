Analyzing Your Survey Data

This tool allows you to view and analyze the responses collected by your surveys. Raw data is useless without analysis. This viewer automatically aggregates your data into visual charts and lists, making it easy to spot trends.

Whether you are looking at a summary of multiple-choice questions or reading individual text responses, this interface provides the tools you need to make sense of the feedback you have received.

🛠️

How to Use the Response Viewer

  1. Log In: To access this viewer and see private survey responses, please log in.
  2. Select a survey from your dashboard to view its data.
  3. Toggle between the Summary tab (charts) and the Individual tab (raw list).
  4. Click Export to download the data as a CSV or PDF file.
✨

Key Features & Benefits

  • Automated Charts: Multiple-choice questions are instantly visualized as bar charts.
  • Real-Time Updates: As new responses come in, the charts update automatically.
  • Filter & Search: Search through text responses to find specific keywords.
  • Secure Access: Only the survey creator can view this page.
💡

Why a Login is Required for Data Access

Survey responses often contain private opinions or personal information submitted by the public. We take data privacy seriously. We require a login for this viewer to:

🔒 Prevent Unauthorized Access

Without authentication, anyone with the survey ID could view the results. Login locks the data to your account.

📈 Generate Custom Reports

We use your account profile to customize the export headers and branding on downloaded PDF reports.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: How do I view responses? ▼
Go to My Surveys, click 'View Responses' on the desired survey. This page will display the data.
Q: Is login required to use the tool? ▼
Yes, a free account is required to access this viewer and see the private answers submitted to your surveys.
Q: Can I delete individual responses? ▼
Yes, in the 'Individual' tab, click the delete icon next to any specific response to remove it from the stats.

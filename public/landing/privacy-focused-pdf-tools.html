🎓 Education

Why Privacy Matters for Your PDFs

PDFs carry more risk than most people realize. Understanding the threats helps you protect yourself — and browser-based tools like InfinityKit are part of the solution.

👻

Hidden Metadata Can Expose You

Every PDF file created by Word, Adobe, or virtually any authoring tool contains invisible metadata: your full name, organization, the software version, creation and modification timestamps, and sometimes even document revision history.

When you submit a PDF to a job application, share a legal document, or publish research, this metadata travels with it — visible to anyone who checks the file properties.

🔒 InfinityKit's Metadata Stripper removes all of this in seconds, privately in your browser, before you share anything.
☁️

Server Uploads Create Real Risk

When you upload a contract, medical record, NDA, or tax return to an online PDF service, you're trusting that company with your sensitive data. Even with good intentions, they face data breach risks, government subpoenas, employee access, and regulatory failures.

Thousands of data breaches happen every year — and they often expose files users thought were "temporarily uploaded" and deleted.

🔒 InfinityKit never receives your files. Zero-upload architecture means zero breach risk for your documents.
⚖️

Compliance Isn't Just a Policy — It's Architecture

GDPR, HIPAA, and similar regulations impose strict requirements on how personal data is handled. Legal teams, healthcare professionals, and HR departments routinely process PDFs that contain regulated data.

A "GDPR-compliant" privacy policy from a server-based service doesn't eliminate risk — it just means they've agreed to try. Browser-based processing eliminates the category of risk entirely.

🔒 InfinityKit's browser-based approach means no data transfer, no controller liability, no regulatory exposure for your document contents.

The Mystery and Utility of Prime Numbers

This tool helps you quickly identify whether a given number is prime or not, which is a fundamental concept in mathematics and number theory. Prime numbers are the building blocks of all positive integers; every number greater than 1 is either a prime itself or can be factored into a unique product of primes.

Beyond pure mathematics, prime numbers play a critical role in modern cybersecurity. The encryption algorithms that secure your bank transactions and private messages (like RSA encryption) rely on the difficulty of factoring large numbers into their prime components. Understanding primes is the key to understanding digital security.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: What is a prime number? ▼
A prime number is a natural number greater than 1 that is divisible only by 1 and itself. Examples include 2, 3, 5, 7, and 11.
Q: Is login required to use the tool? ▼
Yes, a free account is required to check numbers larger than 1,000,000 or save your discovery history. Basic checks are free.
Q: Is 1 a prime number? ▼
No, by definition, prime numbers must be greater than 1. Therefore, 1 is neither prime nor composite.

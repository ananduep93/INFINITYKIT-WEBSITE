Mastering Numbers: LCM and HCF

This tool allows you to find both the Least Common Multiple (LCM) and Highest Common Factor (HCF) of a set of numbers quickly and easily. These two concepts are fundamental in arithmetic and algebra, forming the basis for working with fractions, ratios, and complex number theory.

The LCM of two or more numbers is the smallest positive integer that is divisible by all of them. The HCF (also known as GCD or Greatest Common Divisor) is the largest positive integer that divides each of the numbers without leaving a remainder. Finding these manually involves listing factors or prime factorization, which can be time-consuming for large numbers.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: What is the difference between HCF and LCM? ▼
HCF is the largest number that divides the given numbers. LCM is the smallest number that is divisible by the given numbers. HCF is always smaller than or equal to the smallest number in the set, while LCM is always larger than or equal to the largest number.

The Power of Time-Boxed Focus

This tool is a Pomodoro-style timer designed to help you work in focused bursts. The Pomodoro Technique was developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. It uses a timer to break work into intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks.

Each interval is known as a *pomodoro*, from the Italian word for 'tomato', after the tomato-shaped kitchen timer that Cirillo used as a university student. This method trains your brain to focus for short periods and helps you resist distractions.

🛠️

How to Use the Focus Timer

  1. Log In: To track your focus statistics over time or to join group focus sessions, please log in.
  2. Choose your task to be accomplished.
  3. Set the timer to 25 minutes (default).
  4. Work on the task until the timer rings.
  5. Take a short break (5 minutes).
  6. After four pomodoros, take a longer break (15-30 minutes).

Why the Pomodoro Technique Works

This method leverages psychology to beat procrastination:

🔴 Lowers the Barrier

It's hard to commit to "working on my thesis all day." It's easy to commit to "working for 25 minutes."

🟢 Prevents Burnout

Forced breaks ensure you step away from the screen, stretch, and rest your eyes before getting exhausted.

⏱️ Creates Urgency

A ticking clock provides a slight, healthy pressure that stops you from aimlessly browsing the web.

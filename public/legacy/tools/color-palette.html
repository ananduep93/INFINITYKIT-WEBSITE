The Science of Color in Design

Great design starts with a harmonious color scheme. Colors evoke emotions and set the tone for your entire project. This tool analyzes your images and extracts the most dominant and aesthetically pleasing colors, helping you build a professional palette in seconds.

Whether you are designing a website, a brand logo, or an interior space, pulling colors from a source image ensures that the tones naturally complement each other. Our algorithm ignores noise and focuses on the hues that define the image's character.

🛠️

How to Use the Palette Architect

  1. Log In: To save your generated color palettes to your profile or export them in advanced design formats, please log in.
  2. Upload an image or drop it into the designated zone.
  3. The tool will instantly analyze the pixels and display the dominant hex codes.
  4. Click any color swatch to copy its Hex code to your clipboard.
  5. Export the entire palette as JSON, CSS variables, or Adobe ASE.

Why a Login is Required for Advanced Formats

Extracting colors from an image is a math-heavy process that runs efficiently in your browser. However, saving those palettes and converting them into specialized formats like Adobe Swatch Exchange (.ASE) requires backend processing. We require a login for these features to:

  • Access Across Devices: Access your saved palettes on your desktop while designing and on your phone while checking references.
  • Community Sharing: Opt-in to share your best palettes with the Infinity Kit community gallery.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: How does it extract colors? ▼
The tool uses a color quantization algorithm to find the most dominant and representative colors in your image.
Q: Is login required to use the tool? ▼
Yes, a free account is required to save your generated color palettes to your profile or to export them in advanced design formats like Adobe Swatch Exchange (ASE). Basic extraction is free.
Q: Does it support PNG and JPEG? ▼
Yes, it supports all standard web image formats including PNG, JPEG, and WebP.

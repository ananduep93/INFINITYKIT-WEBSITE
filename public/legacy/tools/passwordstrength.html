The Science of Password Strength

This tool helps you analyze the strength of your passwords, giving you an immediate score and feedback on how to make them more secure against hacking attempts. Password strength is a measure of how difficult it is for a computer program (or a human) to guess or crack the password through brute force or dictionary attacks.

Strength is determined by entropy, which is a mathematical measure of randomness. A password with high entropy has many possible combinations, making it computationally expensive and time-consuming for an attacker to guess. Our checker analyzes length, character variety, and common patterns to give you a realistic assessment.

What Makes a Password Strong?

Follow these guidelines to create passwords that score a "Strong" rating on our meter:

📏 Length is King

Aim for at least 12 to 16 characters. Every additional character exponentially increases the difficulty of cracking it.

🔀 Mix Character Types

Include uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special symbols (like @, #, $).

🚫 Avoid Predictability

Do not use your name, birthdate, or common words found in the dictionary. Randomness is your best friend.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: Is my password saved? ▼
No, your password is only processed locally in your browser to check its strength; it is never stored or sent anywhere.

Q: What is a dictionary attack? ▼

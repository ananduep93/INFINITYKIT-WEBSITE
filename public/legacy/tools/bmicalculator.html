Understanding Body Mass Index (BMI)

This tool calculates your Body Mass Index (BMI) using your height and weight, providing an instant assessment of your body weight relative to your height. BMI is a widely used screening tool to identify whether a person is underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese. It is a simple calculation that correlates moderately well with more direct measures of body fat.

While BMI is not a perfect diagnostic tool (as it does not distinguish between muscle mass and fat mass), it serves as a useful starting point for understanding your general health status and identifying potential weight-related health risks.

Understanding the Categories

Once you calculate your BMI, your score will fall into one of the following standard categories defined by the World Health Organization (WHO):

  • Below 18.5: Underweight. This may indicate malnutrition or other health issues.
  • 18.5 – 24.9: Healthy Weight. This range is associated with the lowest risk of weight-related diseases.
  • 25.0 – 29.9: Overweight. This range increases the risk of heart disease and other conditions.
  • 30.0 or Above: Obese. This indicates a high risk of serious health problems.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: What is BMI? ▼
BMI is a measure that uses your height and weight to work out if your weight is healthy. It is a general indicator and not a diagnostic tool for body fatness or health.
Q: Is BMI accurate for everyone? ▼
BMI is a useful general guide for most adults. However, it has limitations. It may not be accurate for athletes or bodybuilders with high muscle mass, pregnant women, or the elderly who have lost muscle mass.

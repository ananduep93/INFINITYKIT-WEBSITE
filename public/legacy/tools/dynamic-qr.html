The Difference Between Static and Dynamic QR Codes

This tool allows you to create dynamic QR codes. A traditional (static) QR code encodes the destination URL directly into the pixel pattern. Once printed, that URL can never be changed. If you make a typo or change your website, the printed QR code becomes a useless doorstop.

Dynamic QR codes solve this problem by encoding a short redirect URL instead. When a user scans the code, they hit our server first, and we instantly forward them to your destination URL. This means you can change the target URL as many times as you want without ever changing the printed code!

🛠️

How to Use the Dynamic QR Generator

  1. Log In: To create and manage dynamic QR codes, please log in. We need to store your redirect links on our database.
  2. Enter the initial target URL you want people to visit.
  3. Customize the design (colors, logo) of your QR code.
  4. Click Generate and download the image.
  5. To change the link later, log back in and edit the destination in your dashboard.

Why a Login is Required for Dynamic Links

Static QR codes can be generated entirely in JavaScript without any server interaction. However, dynamic QR codes require a backend infrastructure to perform the redirection. We require a login for these features to:

  • Identify Ownership: We need to know which QR code belongs to you so only you can edit its destination.
  • Prevent Phishing: To prevent bad actors from using our domain for malicious redirects, we require user authentication and link scanning.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: What is a dynamic QR code? ▼
A dynamic QR code allows you to change the destination URL after the code has been generated and printed.
Q: Is login required to use the tool? ▼
Yes, a free account is required to create and manage dynamic QR codes because we need to store the redirect links on our server.
Q: Do the links ever expire? ▼
No, as long as your account is active, your dynamic QR codes will continue to redirect users forever.

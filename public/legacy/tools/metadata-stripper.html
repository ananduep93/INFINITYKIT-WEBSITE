Protecting Your Privacy by Stripping Metadata

This tool helps you remove hidden metadata from your files and images before sharing them online. Every photo you take with a smartphone or digital camera contains hidden information called Exif data. This can include the exact GPS coordinates of where the photo was taken, the date and time, and the device model.

While this data is useful for photographers, it poses a significant privacy risk when sharing photos on social media or public marketplaces. Our Metadata Stripper removes all this hidden information, leaving you with a clean file that is safe to share.

🛠️

How to Use the Metadata Stripper

  1. Log In: To process files larger than 10MB or batch process multiple files at once, please log in or create a free account.
  2. Upload the image or file you want to clean by clicking the upload area.
  3. The tool will automatically detect and list the metadata found in the file.
  4. Click Strip Metadata to create a clean copy of the file.
  5. Download your privacy-protected file.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: Does it reduce image quality? ▼
No, removing metadata only removes the hidden text data and does not compress or alter the visual pixels of the image.
Q: Is login required to use the tool? ▼
Yes, a free account is required to process files larger than 10MB or batch process multiple files. Basic stripping for small files is free.
Q: What specific data is removed? ▼
We remove Exif data (camera settings), IPTC data (copyright and keywords), and XMP data (editing history), including GPS coordinates.

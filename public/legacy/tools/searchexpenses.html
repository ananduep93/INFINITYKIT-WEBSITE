The Power of Quick Retrieval

This tool allows you to quickly locate specific expense entries from your history using keywords, categories, or other identifying information. As your financial ledger grows over months and years, finding a specific receipt or transaction becomes like looking for a needle in a haystack.

Manual scrolling is inefficient and frustrating. A powerful search function turns your massive list of numbers into a searchable database, allowing you to answer questions like "How much did I spend at that specific restaurant last March?" in seconds.

🛠️

How to Use the Search Tool

  1. Log In: To search across all connected devices or use advanced filters (like amount ranges), please log in.
  2. Enter a keyword, store name, or category into the search bar.
  3. The list below will filter automatically as you type.
  4. Click on a result to see the full details of that transaction.
✨

Key Features & Benefits

  • Real-Time Filtering: Results update instantly with every letter you type.
  • Multi-Field Search: Searches through notes, categories, and tags simultaneously.
  • Date Range Picker: Narrow down results to a specific week or month.
  • Export Results: Logged-in users can export the search results as a separate CSV file.
💡

When is Expense Search Useful?

Consider these common scenarios where searching saves time:

🧾 Tax Season

Search for "Donation" or "Business Expense" to quickly find all tax-deductible receipts from the past year.

🔄 Subscription Audit

Search for terms like "Netflix" or "Spotify" to see exactly how much you are spending on digital services monthly.

🛡️ Warranty Claims

Find the exact date you purchased an electronic item by searching for the product name to see if it is still under warranty.

🚀

Why a Login is Required for Advanced Search

Basic keyword search runs locally on the data stored in your current browser. However, if you want to find an expense you logged on your phone while looking at your laptop, that data must be queried from our servers. We require a login for these features to:

  • Enable Cross-Device Queries: Search the unified cloud database rather than just the local machine.
  • Run Complex Filters: Server-side processing handles heavy calculations like "expenses between $50 and $100 in the groceries category" efficiently.

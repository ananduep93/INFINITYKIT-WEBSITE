The Geometry of Triangles

This tool helps you quickly identify the type of a triangle based on the lengths of its three sides, making geometry problems easier to solve. In mathematics, a triangle is a polygon with three edges and three vertices. It is one of the basic shapes in geometry.

However, not just any three numbers can form a triangle. According to the **Triangle Inequality Theorem**, the sum of the lengths of any two sides must be greater than the length of the remaining side. This tool automatically checks that rule first before classifying the triangle.

Understanding Triangle Classifications

Triangles are classified based on the relationships between their side lengths:

📐 Equilateral

All three sides are of equal length. All interior angles are also equal (60 degrees each).

📏 Isosceles

Two sides are of equal length, and the angles opposite those sides are also equal.

📉 Scalene

All three sides have different lengths. All interior angles also have different measures.
